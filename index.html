Section 01

Executive Summary

UNICAF University currently operates student registration, fee collection, and bank reconciliation as separate manual workflows. Students navigate between departments, bank branches, and university offices to complete processes that should be seamless. This fragmentation increases administrative overhead, slows enrolment cycles, and creates a frustrating student experience.

We propose the UNICAF Integrated Student Services Platform (ISSP) — a two-part solution that directly addresses the challenges raised during our initial engagement:

⬡

Aggregation Engine

A central middleware layer that interconnects your Student Information System (SIS), banking partners, and internal workflows into one unified platform — so every registration, payment, and receipt flows through a single system of record.

☎

USSD Payment Channel

A mobile-native, internet-independent payment channel accessible via short code on any handset. Students dial in, authenticate, select their fee category, and pay directly from their mobile money or bank account — no app or data connection required.

The combined platform will reduce UNICAF's student onboarding cycle, eliminate manual bank reconciliation, and extend fee collection to 100% of the student population — including those in areas with limited internet connectivity.

Section 02

Understanding Your Needs

During our meeting, UNICAF's team identified two core operational pain points that current systems fail to address. We have captured these below and mapped them to specific solution components.

1

Pain Point: Fragmented Student Journey

Students currently move between registration offices, finance counters, and bank branches to complete enrolment. There is no single system linking their academic registration to their fee payment status. This creates delays, data discrepancies, and a poor experience.

2

Pain Point: Limited Payment Accessibility

Fee payments currently depend on bank branch visits or online banking — both of which exclude students in rural or under-connected areas. UNICAF requires a payment channel that works on basic feature phones without internet, accessible via USSD short codes.

Section 03

Proposed Solution Overview

The UNICAF Integrated Student Services Platform is designed around two functional pillars that work together as a single ecosystem.

Pillar 1 — Aggregation Engine

The Aggregation Engine acts as the central nervous system of UNICAF's student lifecycle. It sits between your existing Student Information System, partner banks, mobile money providers, and internal departments — routing data, triggering workflows, and maintaining a single source of truth for every student transaction.

Core capabilities include real-time student identity verification during registration, automated fee assessment and billing tied to programme and semester, multi-bank payment collection with instant reconciliation, automated receipt generation and delivery via SMS, unified reporting dashboard for finance and registrar teams, and a RESTful API layer enabling future integrations with any third-party system.

Pillar 2 — USSD Payment System

The USSD channel provides a universally accessible payment interface through a dedicated short code (e.g. *384#). Students on any GSM handset — smartphone or basic feature phone — can access their fee balance, initiate payments via mobile money or bank-linked accounts, receive instant SMS confirmation, and view transaction history. The USSD gateway communicates directly with the Aggregation Engine, ensuring every transaction is reflected in the central platform within seconds.

Section 07

Implementation Roadmap

We propose a phased delivery model that allows UNICAF to begin realising value from Phase 1 while subsequent phases are under development. Total estimated delivery timeline is 16–20 weeks from contract signature.

Phase 1 — Weeks 1–4
Discovery & Foundation
Technical audit of UNICAF's existing SIS and banking relationships. API specification design. Infrastructure provisioning. USSD short code registration with MNOs. Stakeholder workshops to finalise fee structures and workflow rules.
Phase 2 — Weeks 5–10
Core Platform Build
Aggregation Engine development including SIS adapter, bank API integrations, and reconciliation logic. Admin web portal build-out with dashboard, transaction management, and student records. USSD session flow development and MNO integration testing.
Phase 3 — Weeks 11–14
Integration & Testing
End-to-end integration testing across all channels. UAT with UNICAF finance and registrar teams. Load testing for peak registration periods. Security audit and penetration testing. MNO USSD approval and live short code activation.
Phase 4 — Weeks 15–16
Go-Live & Handover
Staged rollout beginning with a pilot cohort. Admin training for finance and registrar staff. Student communication campaign (SMS blast with USSD instructions). Transition to production support. Performance monitoring and optimisation.
